But summer presents its own set of challenges. A few of the most difficult things are heat stress, drought, increased pest activity, and storm damage from sudden summer thunderstorms. All of these can take a toll, especially on older or already weakened trees. That’s why early summer is the perfect time to take action. We believe that proactive care is the best way to avoid costly problems later in the season.</p><h2>Pruning for Summer Safety and Health</h2><p>Pruning isn’t just a winter or spring activity. During early summer, light pruning can help reduce the weight of overgrown limbs, improve airflow through the canopy, and minimize the risk of storm damage. We target first and foremost, dead or dying branches that attract insects and could cause damage if they fall. Secondly, our tree experts will look for limbs that overhang structures or power lines and prune them accordingly. As a locally owned company, we know West Creek’s tree species and how they respond to pruning at different times of year. Our team works with care and precision to avoid stressing your trees during warmer weather.</p><h2>Mulching and Watering Tips from West Creek Tree Companies</h2><p>Another common summer issue in West Creek is moisture retention. Many yards struggle with sandy or compacted soil that doesn’t hold water well. If your trees look wilted or the ground around them is dry just a few hours after rain, it may be time for some help.</p><p>We often recommend:</p><ul><li>Mulching with 2–3 inches of organic mulch (like shredded bark), keeping it 3 inches away from the trunk to avoid rot</li><li>Deep watering 1–2 times per week, allowing water to soak 6–12 inches into the root zone</li><li>Root zone inspection to detect signs of compaction or root damage</li></ul><p>Our team can help you set up a watering plan and even treat stressed trees with deep-root fertilization if needed.</p><h2>Keeping an Eye Out for Summer Pests</h2><p>As one of the top-rated West Creek tree companies, we’ve seen our fair share of pest issues — from <a href="https://extension.umd.edu/resource/bagworms-trees-and-shrubs/">bagworms</a> in evergreens to <a href="https://plantaddicts.com/maple-tree-pests/?srsltid=AfmBOoryu7mItk1buankzLToLfEKyt489ue3cQa2SOr5p-GZfg_hoqtw">aphids</a> in maples. These pests tend to thrive in the heat and can quickly spread if not addressed early. One obvious sign of an infestation is sticky sap or “honeydew” on leaves or cars parked nearby. Additionally, discoloration or wilting of leaves despite proper watering is a clear warning sign. Yet another example of red flags for pests are unusual holes in bark or frass (sawdust) at the base of the tree. If any of these or a number of warning signs appear, we will use a targeted, environmentally safe treatment when needed and always start with the least invasive solution possible.</p><h2>Trust Local Pros of West Creek Tree Companies This Summer</h2><p>When it comes to West Creek tree companies, you have options — but few offer the combination of experience, professionalism, and personal service that we do.
