West Creek Tree Companies: Preparing Your Trees for Summer

As one of the most trusted West Creek tree companies, we’ve seen firsthand how the changing seasons can affect your trees. We also understand how the right care at the right time can make all the difference. At Ben Bivins Tree Experts, we’re proud to serve the West Creek community with professional tree care solutions year-round, but today, we want to talk about why summer prep matters and how to keep your trees healthy, strong, and safe through the hottest months of the year.

Why Summer Prep Is Critical for Tree Health

It’s easy to assume that once spring has done its job, your trees are good to go. But summer presents its own set of challenges. A few of the most difficult things are heat stress, drought, increased pest activity, and storm damage from sudden summer thunderstorms. All of these can take a toll, especially on older or already weakened trees. That’s why early summer is the perfect time to take action. We believe that proactive care is the best way to avoid costly problems later in the season.

Pruning for Summer Safety and Health

Pruning isn’t just a winter or spring activity. During early summer, light pruning can help reduce the weight of overgrown limbs, improve airflow through the canopy, and minimize the risk of storm damage. We target first and foremost, dead or dying branches that attract insects and could cause damage if they fall. Secondly, our tree experts will look for limbs that overhang structures or power lines and prune them accordingly. As a locally owned company, we know West Creek’s tree species and how they respond to pruning at different times of year. Our team works with care and precision to avoid stressing your trees during warmer weather.

Mulching and Watering Tips from West Creek Tree Companies

Another common summer issue in West Creek is moisture retention. Many yards struggle with sandy or compacted soil that doesn’t hold water well. If your trees look wilted or the ground around them is dry just a few hours after rain, it may be time for some help.

We often recommend:

  • Mulching with 2–3 inches of organic mulch (like shredded bark), keeping it 3 inches away from the trunk to avoid rot
  • Deep watering 1–2 times per week, allowing water to soak 6–12 inches into the root zone
  • Root zone inspection to detect signs of compaction or root damage

Our team can help you set up a watering plan and even treat stressed trees with deep-root fertilization if needed.

Keeping an Eye Out for Summer Pests

As one of the top-rated West Creek tree companies, we’ve seen our fair share of pest issues — from bagworms in evergreens to aphids in maples. These pests tend to thrive in the heat and can quickly spread if not addressed early. One obvious sign of an infestation is sticky sap or “honeydew” on leaves or cars parked nearby. Additionally, discoloration or wilting of leaves despite proper watering is a clear warning sign. Yet another example of red flags for pests are unusual holes in bark or frass (sawdust) at the base of the tree. If any of these or a number of warning signs appear, we will use a targeted, environmentally safe treatment when needed and always start with the least invasive solution possible.
